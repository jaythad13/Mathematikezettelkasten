### Graphs on a surface

Graphs of genus $0$ can be drawn on the plane (without crossings), and thus, by stereographic projection, can be drawn on the sphere, a surface of genus $0$.

Similarly, graphs of genus $1$ (which can't be drawn on the plane without crossing) can be drawn on the genus $1$ surface of the torus.

### Belyi pairs and Dessin d'Enfant

##### _theorem, definition:_ Belyi pairs (Weil, Belyi) 

If $S$ is a compact, connected Riemann surface, then it is a smooth, irreducible algebraic variety. If $S$ is the zero locus of $f \in \mathbb{C}[x, y]$ with algebraic coefficients, then there is a rational function $\beta : S \to \mathbb{P}^1(\mathbb{C})$. If there is a rational function $\beta : S \to \mathbb{P}^1(\mathbb{C})$, then $S$ is a smooth, irreducible algebraic variety.

The pair $(S, \beta)$ is called a Belyi pair
