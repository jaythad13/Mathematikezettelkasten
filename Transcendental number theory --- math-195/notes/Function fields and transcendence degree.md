Let $\mathbb{F}$ be a field. The function fields over $\mathbb{F}$ are important because they are the model for all [[Transcendental number theory --- math-195/notes/Transcendental and algebraic numbers#Transcendental extensions|transcendental extensions]].

##### _definition:_ field of rational functions

We define the **field of rational functions over $\mathbb{F}$ in $n$ variables** to be $\mathbb{F}(x_{1}, \dots, x_{n})$, the [[Commutative algebra --- math-189AA/notes/Localisation of a ring#_definition _ localisation of a ring, localisations at a prime, fraction field|fraction field]] of the ring of polynomials $\mathbb{F}[x_{1}, \dots, x_{n}]$.
