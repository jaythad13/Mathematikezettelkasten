We've seen this idea so many times before in homework, but obviously it's worth defining it explicitly. A subgroup is just a group sitting inside the group with the same operation.

##### _definition:_ subgroup, $\le$

Let $G$ be a group. A subset $H \subset G$ is a subgroup if
1) $H$ is nonempty
2) for any $x, y \in H$ we have $xy \in H$
3) if $x \in H$ then $x^{-1} \in H$

If $H$ is a subgroup of $G$, then we have $H \le G$

##### _definition:_ proper subgroup

If $H \le G$ but $H \neq G$, we say $H$ is a proper subgroup of $G$ and we write $H < G$.

##### _proposition:_ subgroups of subgroups are subgroups

If $H \le G$ and $K \le H$ then $K \le H$. That is, $\le$ as a relation on the class of groups, is transitive.

##### _proof sketch:_

Basically everything follows from the definition of $K \le H$.

Note that then $\le$ is a transitive, reflexive (since $G \le G$) relation on the class of groups. Thus, this forms a partial order on the class of groups. We can also look at the partial order generated by the subgroups of a specific group.

##### _example:_ the lattice of subgroups of $\mathbb{Z} / 6 \mathbb{Z}$

We draw a Hasse diagram
```tikz
\usepackage{tikz-cd}
\usepackage{amsfonts}
\begin{document}
	\begin{tikzcd}
		& \mathbb Z/6 \mathbb Z = \left < \bar{1} \right > = \left < \bar{5} \right > \\
		\left < \bar{2} \right > = \left < \bar{4} \right > \ar[ru] & & \left < \bar{3} \right > \ar[lu] \\
		& \left < 0 \right > \ar[lu] \ar[ru]
	\end{tikzcd}
\end{document}
```

Similarly

##### _examples:_ subgroup lattices of $C_{4}$, $\mathbb{Z} / 8 \mathbb{Z}$, $Q_{8}$

The cyclic group of order $4$, $C_{4}$ (we choose the representation in $\mathbb{C}$) has subgroups as

```tikz
\usepackage{tikz-cd}
\usepackage{amsfonts}
\begin{document}
	\begin{tikzcd}
		C_4 = \langle i \rangle = \langle -i \rangle \\
		\langle -1 \rangle \ar[u] \\
		\langle 1 \rangle \ar[u]
	\end{tikzcd}
\end{document}
```

$\mathbb{Z} / 8 \mathbb{Z}$ has the subgroup lattice below

```tikz
\usepackage{tikz-cd}
\usepackage{amsfonts}
\begin{document}
	\begin{tikzcd}
		\mathbb Z / 8 \mathbb Z = \langle \bar 1 \rangle = \langle \bar 3 \rangle = \langle \bar 5 \rangle = \langle \bar 7 \rangle \\
		\langle \bar 2 \rangle = \langle 6 \rangle \ar[u] \\
		\langle \bar 4 \rangle \ar[u] \\
		\langle \bar 0 \rangle \ar[u]
	\end{tikzcd}
\end{document}
```


The [[Matrix groups and the quaternions#_definition _ the quaternions|the quaternions]] have the following lattice

```tikz
\usepackage{tikz-cd}
\usepackage{amsfonts}
\begin{document}
	\begin{tikzcd}
		& Q_8 \\
		\langle \pm i \rangle \ar[ru] & \langle \pm j \rangle \ar[u] & \langle \pm k \rangle \ar[lu] \\
		& \langle -1 \rangle \ar[ru] \ar[u] \ar[lu] \\
		& \langle 1 \rangle \ar[u]
	\end{tikzcd}
\end{document}
```
