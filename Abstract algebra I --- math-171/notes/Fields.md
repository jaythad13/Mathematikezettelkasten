Fields are a special type of commutative ring with identity.

##### _definition:_ fields

A ring with multiplicative identity $1 \neq 0$ where every nonzero element has a multiplicative inverse, and the multiplication is commutative is called a field.

##### _example:_ fields

1) $\mathbb{Z}/n\mathbb{Z}$ is a field if and only if $n$ is prime
2) $\mathbb{Q}$, the rational numbers
3) $\mathbb{F}_{2}$ is the unique finite field of order $2$. It is just $\{ 0, 1 \}$.

##### _facts:_ about fields!

We may not prove all of these in this class

1) $\mathbb{Z} / n \mathbb{Z}$ [[Superdiscrete --- math-55A/notes/Modular arithmetic#_corollary _ $ bb{Z}_p$ is a field, whatever that means|is a field if and only if]] $n$ is prime.
2) If $\mathbb{F}$ is a finite field then $\lvert \mathbb{F} \rvert = p^n$ for some prime $p$ and some $n \in \mathbb{N}$.
3) There is exactly one field (upto isomorphism) of order $p^n$ where $p$ is any prime and $n$ is any natural number, denoted $\mathbb{F}_{p^n}$.
