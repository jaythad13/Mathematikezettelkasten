For brevity, when we say Alexandroff space, we mean finite [[UChicago --- uc-2026/notes/Alexandroff spaces and posets#_definition _ Alexandroff spaces, A-spaces|A-space]]. Note that this suffices to classify all finite (Alexandroff) spaces, since there is a deformation retract from a finite space to its $T_{0}$ Kolmogorov quotient. We consider an Alexandroff space simultaneously as a topological space and a [[UChicago --- uc-2026/notes/Alexandroff spaces and posets#_proposition _ the preorder on an Alexandroff space|poset]].

We can give a poset criterion for two maps of Alexandroff spaces to be homotopic.

##### _lemma:_ paths in Alexandroff spaces

For each $x_{1} \leq x_{2}$ in an Alexandroff space $X$, there is a path $\alpha : [0, 1] \to X$ from $x_{1}$ to $x_{2}$.

---

##### _definition:_ compact open topology

The **compact open topology** on $\operatorname{Mor}_{\mathsf{Top}}(X, Y)$ is the topology generated by the [[Topology --- math-147/notes/Subbases#_definition _ subbasis|subbasis]] of $f : X \to Y$ such that, for each compact $A \subseteq X$, we have $f^\text{img}(A) \subseteq U$ for some open $U \subseteq Y$. 

---

Note that this topology gives an adjunction $\operatorname{Mor}(X \times Y, Z) \cong \operatorname{Mor}(X, \operatorname{Mor}(Y, Z))$ for locally path-connected spaces $X$ (including metric spaces, Alexandroff spaces, et c.). This is also true in the category of compactly generated spaces (which was defined exactly so that this adjunction holds).

In particular, a homotopy $X \times [0, 1] \to Y$ is the same as a path $[0, 1] \to \operatorname{Mor}(X, Y)$.

##### _proposition:_ the morphism-set is an Alexandroff space

For any $f, g \in \operatorname{Mor}(X, Y)$, consider the [[Topology --- math-147/notes/Subbases#_definition _ order topology|order topology]] defined by the poset with $f \leq g$ when $f(x) \leq g(x)$ for each $x \in X$. This is the same as the compact open topology on $\operatorname{Mor}(X, Y)$.

---

Thus, by the adjunction above, we have a sufficient condition for [[Algebraic topology --- concise/notes/Homotopy#_definition _ homotopic, homotopy, homotopic relative to|homotopies]] of maps between Alexandroff spaces.

##### _proposition:_ homotopies of paths

Suppose $f \leq g \in \operatorname{Mor}(X, Y)$. Then $f \simeq g$.

---

The idea here is that we can reduce to a minimal subspace that is in fact unique!

##### _definition:_

The core of a finite space is a minimal subspace that is a [[VSRP colloquium --- vsrp/notes/Simple homotopy equivalences#_example _ mapping cylinder, strong deformation retract|deformation retract]] of it.
