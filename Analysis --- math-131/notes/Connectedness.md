Connectedness is an intuitive concept but hard to define. Again $(X, d)$ is a metric space. 

##### _definition:_ separated

We say $A, B \subset X$ are separated if $\overline{A} \cap B = \emptyset =  A \cap \overline{B}$. 

Note that this is stricter than just requiring that $A$ and $B$ are disjoint — none of the limit points of $A$ can be in $B$ and vice versa.

##### _example:_ disjoint is not the same as separated

$(0, 1)$ and $[1, 2)$ are disjoint but not separated. However, $(0, 1)$ and $(1, 2)$ are separated!

##### _definition:_ connectedness

We say $E \subset X$ is connected if there are no separated $A, B \subset E$ such that $E = A \cup B$.
