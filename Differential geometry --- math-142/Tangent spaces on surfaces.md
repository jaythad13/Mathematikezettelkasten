### Directions on a surface

If $D \subset \mathbb{R}^2$ is open with a mapping $x : D \to \mathbb{R}^3$, we can draw a line around each point $(u_{0}, v_{0})$ and then push it through to the image $x^\text{img}(D)$. That is, we have the curve $\alpha : I \to D \subset \mathbb{R}^2$ by $\alpha(t) = (u_{0} + at, v_{0} + bt)$ contained in $D$. Thus, $x \circ \alpha$ is a map $I \to \mathbb{R}$.
