##### _theorem:_ Bezout's theorem

Suppose $X, Y \subseteq \mathbb{C} \mathbb{P}^2$ are [[Algebraic curves and Riemann surfaces --- math-199DR/notes/Affine and projective algebraic curves#_definition _ projective plane curve|projective plane curves]] defined by smooth, homogeneous polynomials $f, g \in \mathbb{C}[x, y, z]$ having no common factors. Then $\# X \cap Y = (\deg f) (\deg g)$.

###### _proof:_

Since $X$ and $Y$ intersect nicely, we have $\# X \cap Y = [X \cap Y] = [X] [Y]$. In particular, complex submanifolds of $\mathbb{C}\mathbb{P}^n$ (or of any complex manifold) [[Algebraic curves and Riemann surfaces --- math-199DR/notes/Complex projective space#_definition _ intersection product on cohomology|intersect with positive orientation]].

Let $i : X \to \mathbb{C} \mathbb{P}^{2}$ be the embedding and let $i_{*}[X] \in H_{2}(\mathbb{C} \mathbb{P}^{2}, \mathbb{Z})$ be the pushforward of the fundamental class. Since $H_{2}(\mathbb{C} \mathbb{P}^{2}, \mathbb{Z})$ is [[Algebraic curves and Riemann surfaces --- math-199DR/notes/Complex projective space#_theorem _ homology of projective space|generated by]] some hyperplane class $[H]$, we have $i_{*}[X] = d [H]$. What is $r$? Choose a line $L$ transverse to $i_{*}[X]$. Then $[L][X]$ is the number of points at which $L$ intersects $X$, which is just $\deg f$. At the same time, $[L]d[H] = d$.

Similarly, $i_{*}[Y] = \deg g[H]$. Thus, $[X] [Y] = (\deg f)(\deg g)$.
