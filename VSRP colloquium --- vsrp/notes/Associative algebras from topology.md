### Topological quantum field theories and Frobenius algebras

##### _definition:_ topological quantum field theories

For each compact oriented $1$-manifold $N$ (these are either disjoint unions of $S^1$ or $\text{Ø}$) assign a functor $\tau$ to (finite-dimensional) vector spaces such that $\tau(N_{1} \sqcup N_{2}) = \tau(N_{1}) \otimes \tau(N_{2})$.

Also assign to every $2$-manifold $M$ with boundary $\partial M = N$ assign a vector $\tau(M)$ in $\tau(N)$. It follows that gluing along holes corresponds to multiplication (because tensor product?).

##### _definition:_ Frobenius algebras

A Frobenius algebra is a finite commutative associative algebra with a unit and a linear map $\operatorname{tr} : A \to \mathbb{C}$ such that the bilinear form $A \times A \to \mathbb{C}$ given by $\left< a, b \right> = \operatorname{tr}(ab)$ is non-degenerate.

### Hatcher–Thurston moves

There are three ways to cut open a $4$-punctured sphere into pairs of pants — the Hatcher–Thurston moves. The Hatcher–Thurston theorem says that these are transitive on the set of pair-of-pants decompositions — you can get from any decomposition to another by gluing two pairs of pants together and recutting the resultant $4$-punctured sphere with a different Hatcher–Thurston move.
