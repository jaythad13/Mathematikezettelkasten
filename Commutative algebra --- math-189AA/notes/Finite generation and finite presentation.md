Let $A$ be a ring.

### Finite generation

Finite generation requires a finite number of generators. This can be framed as requiring a surjection from the finite-dimensional free-module generated by those generators.

##### _definition:_ finitely generated

An $A$-module $M$ is **finitely generated** if there is an exact sequence
```tikz
\usepackage{tikz-cd}
\usepackage{amsfonts}
\begin{document}
	\begin{tikzcd}
		A^{\oplus m} \ar[r, two heads, "\varepsilon"] & M \ar[r] & 0.
	\end{tikzcd}
\end{document}
```

### Finite presentation

Finite presentation requires finitely many generators and also requires a finite number of relations. This can be framed as requiring the surjection from the finite-dimensional free module to have finitely generated kernel.

##### _definition:_ finitely presented

A module is **finitely presented** if there is an exact sequence
```tikz
\usepackage{tikz-cd}
\usepackage{amsfonts}
\begin{document}
	\begin{tikzcd}
		A^{\oplus m} \ar[r] & A^{\oplus n} \ar[r, "\varepsilon", two heads] & M \ar[r] & 0.
	\end{tikzcd}
\end{document}
```
