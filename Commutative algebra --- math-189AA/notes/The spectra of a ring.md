A ring has two spectra — the prime spectrum and maximal spectrum. 

##### _definition:_ prime spectrum

The prime spectrum of a ring $A$ is $\operatorname{Spec} A$, the set of [[Abstract algebra --- math-171/notes/Prime and maximal ideals#_definition _ prime ideals|prime ideals]], ordered by inclusion, with the Zariski [[Topology --- math-147/notes/Topologies#_definition _ topology, open sets, topological space|topology]] — the topology defined by declaring closed sets to be $V(\mathfrak{i}) = \{ \mathfrak{p} \in \operatorname{Spec} A | \mathfrak{p} \supseteq \mathfrak{i} \}$ for ideals $\mathfrak{i} \subseteq A$.

---

We prove this is a topology in [[Commutative algebra --- math-189AA/attachments/homework/hw 1/hw 1.pdf#page=9|the homework]]. We will only rarely use the topology on $\operatorname{Spec} A$.

##### _definition:_ maximal spectrum

The maximal spectrum of a ring $A$ is $\operatorname{mSpec} A$, the set of [[Abstract algebra --- math-171/notes/Prime and maximal ideals#_definition _ maximal ideal|maximal ideals]], ordered by inclusion.
