Group schemes allow us to make sense of the natural scheme structure that groups like $\mathrm{GL}_{n}$ should have, as well as the natural group structure that some schemes like $\mathbb{A}^1$ should have. In fancy language, they are group objects in the $\mathsf{Sch}$, or fancier still, they are [[Algebraic geometry --- rising-sea/notes/Representable functors of schemes|representable functors]] that factor through $\mathsf{Grp}$.
