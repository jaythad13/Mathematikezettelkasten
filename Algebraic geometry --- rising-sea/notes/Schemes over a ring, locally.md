Let $A$ be a ring, $\mathbb{F}$ be a field.

It will be important to us to have a definition of a $\mathbb{F}$-scheme, or more generally an $A$-scheme. Once we know what morphisms of schemes are, we will see that these are special cases of the relative notion of $S$-schemes for an arbitrary base scheme $S$. However, we can also build $A$-schemes by gluing together $A$-algebras, which we will do here.
