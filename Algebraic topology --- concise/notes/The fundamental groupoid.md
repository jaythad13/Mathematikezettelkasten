We have a nice enough understanding of how the [[Algebraic topology --- concise/notes/The fundamental group#_definition _ fundamental group|fundamental group]] [[Algebraic topology --- concise/notes/Homotopy group calculations#_proposition _ homotopy groups are invariant on path components|depends]] on a choice of base point. However, dealing with a choice of base point all the time can get in the way of stating theorems naturally, particularly [[Algebraic topology --- concise/notes/Seifert-van Kampen's theorem|the Seifert-van Kampen theorem]]. We define the fundamental groupoid to get around this.
